What is personality?

Personality refers to individual differences in thinking, feeling, and behaving that remain relatively stable over an extended period of time and are consistent across different environments and situations, (Haslam & Smilie, 2022).

Why should we be fussed about personality?

Topics around personality have taken place, back in 319 BC the tremendous Theophrastus pondered “Often before now have I applied my thoughts to the puzzling questions – why it is that , while all Greece lies under the same sky and all the Greeks are educated alike, It has befallen us to have characters so variously constituted”. Whether it be over tea and biscuits with friends or catching up on the latest gossip with work colleagues in the lunchroom, we have probably all attempted to describe our own or another person's fundamental tendencies. We are social animals that are motivated to understand how we are like all people, like some people, or like no other person, Murray, and Kluckhohn (1953).

Personality Traits

In psychology personality traits are often used as the descriptive unit of personality. A personality trait is quite specific in definition, such that a trait must be" .

  • Psychological not physical: Refers to psychological phenomena, e.g. kind not tall.
  • Enduring not transient: Refers to an enduring trait consistent across time and situation, and not a fleeting emotion. e.g. warm not sad.
  • Broad not specific: Broadly describes behaviours across a number of situations. E.g. Disciplined not pizza lover.
  • sociable, assertive or enthusiastic (Mid-level traits describing extraversion).
