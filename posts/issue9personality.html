What is personality?

Personality refers to individual differences in thinking, feeling, and behaving that are relatively stable over a long period of time and remain consistent across different environments and situations (Haslam & Smilie, 2022).

Personality Traits

Personality traits are often used as the descriptive unit of personality. In psychology a personality trait is quite specific and must follow a few rules (Haslam & Smilie, 2022).

Principle Description
✔ Psychological not physical Refers to psychological phenomena, e.g. kind not Tall.
✔ Enduring not transient Refers to an enduring trait consistent across time and situation, not a fleeting emotion. E.g. warm not sad.
✔ Broad not specific Broadly describes behaviours across a number of situations. E.g. disciplined not pizza lover.

How did we get to the BIG 5 (OCEAN)

Amazing Allport and Outstanding Odbert

You may be wondering how is this better than types? I am glad you ask the BIG 5 has been in development since 1936 and is a result using methods like statistical reduction. Allport & Odbert kickbelieved that anything of value or important enough to a society is encoded in language. In 1936 the boys took a New International Dictionary in search of an extensive list of possible adjectives that could be used to describe individual differences. The criteria to make it on there list was as follows.

  • Describes broad ways in which people can be different
  • Describes temporary moods or activities.
  • Describes social or character judgements of personal conduct.
  • Describes of physique, mental capacities and developmental conditions.
Out popped the small list of 17,953 traits like impulisive, methodical and sociable + another 17,950 of a similar tune.

Remarkable Raymond Cattell

Like Allport & Odbert, Cattell supported the lexical hypothesis, but also hypothesised that personality traits correlate with real world outcomes. he was able to reduce Allports & Odberts list of 17, 953 trait into 16 fundamental personality factors using statistical methods like clustering and factor analysis.

Legendary Lewis R. Goldberg

Goldy in 1993 published "An alternative description of personality: The Big-Five factor structure" research showed across decades of psychology research the BIG 5 personality factors constantly emerged, with the above studies being landmark findings of the time. The BIG 5 factors also holds up across cultures, with some minor differences culturally specific factors sometimes emerge e.g. using the same appraoch 'Harmony' arises in Chinese. But fundamentally the BIG 5 holds up acorss cultures.
