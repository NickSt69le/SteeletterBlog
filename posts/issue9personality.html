What is personality?

Personality describes stable patterns of thinking, feeling, and behaving that remain relatively constant across different environments, situations and over an extended period of time, often spanning years and decades, (Haslam & Smilie, 2022).

THE BIG 5 (OCEAN)üî•

The BIG 5 provides a structured hierarchy of personality traits. The high-level traits capture the five fundamental domains of individual differeneces. As you move down the hierarchy, the personality traits become more specific in definition, eventually leading to some kind of observable behaviour associated with the trait. Like you can move from a high-level trait to an observable behaviour, it is also possible to move bottom up from a specific instance to a broad level trait. For example, if you describe someone as Neurotic, it is very broad in definition and doesn't reveal much about what to expect. The use of a mid-level trait, such as nervous, would you a much better idea of what you might be in for if you bumped into this hypothetical person.

BIG 5 Historyüìú

The BIG 5 has been in work since 1936, and is the result of massive amounts of research that uses statistical methods such as clustering and factor analysis over many large datasets and seminal works. The BIG 5 evolved on the basis of the lexical hypothesis, the idea that anything of value or important enough to a society is given a word and encoded in language

Figure: BIG 5 key events

Amazing Allport and Outstanding Odbert

In 1936, Allport & Odbert dusted off a New International Dictionary and set out to extract all in search of words that described individual differences. Inclusion criteria for their list. Describes broad ways in which people can be different Describes temporary moods or activities. Describes social or character judgments of personal conduct. Descriptions of physique, mental capacities, and developmental conditions. Their easter egg hunt resulted in a list of 4,500 traits like impulsive, methodical, and sociable.

Remarkable Raymond Cattell

Cattell used Allport's and Odbert's list of 4500 trait terms into 16 fundamental personality factors using statistical methods like clustering and factor analysis. He published the 16PF inventory, which boasts strong test-retest reliability - Taking the test at time point one will yield scores ~85‚Äì94% similar if taken the same test again 2 weeks later. The 16PF comes loaded with predictive validity, identifying quality leaders ~60‚Äì68% of the time (Conn et ., 1994). This isn't perfect, but it is still better than flipping a coin.

Legendary Lewis R. Goldberg

Goldy in 1993 published "An alternative description of personality: The Big-Five factor structure" research showed across decades of psychology research the BIG 5 personality factors constantly emerged and BIG 5 was born.

Standardised Inventories

Before wrapping it up, it wouldn't be right for me to soil on the Myers-Briggs Nonsense Inventory without providing alternatives. Common instruments include Cattell's 16PF, NEO-PI-R and the MMPI, which have undergone decades of research and testing and boast solid reliability and validity metrics. These tests have been completed by large populations of people, meaning that your results are compared to the established norms of most other people. . The MMPI, however, is reserved for qualified psychologists and psychiatrists because it is tailored for clinical assessment and high-risk occupational contexts. Some of its features include inbuilt mechanisms to catch fibsters, attempts to ‚Äúfake good‚Äù and conversely ‚Äúfake bad‚Äù , infrequent and random responses. For example, if someone has approached the questionnaire in a way that makes them appear more positive than they actually are, the MMPI can identify this. Because all the survey items have a known statistical relationship, correction can be applied, revealing a more accurate psychological profile of the person under test.
